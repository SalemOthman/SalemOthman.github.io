About Me

Research

My research interests span across Big Data, Machine Learning, and Social Network Analysis. I am particularly passionate about exploring the intricacies and potential of Large Language Models (LLMs) and their applications in various fields.

Key Research Projects

  • Emojiset Mining Research: A project focused on understanding the contextual and emotional nuances conveyed by emojis in digital communication. This research has led to the development of www.sogoresearch.com, a platform for detailed emoji analysis.
  • Epidemiology Research: This project involves leveraging Big Data and Machine Learning techniques to better understand epidemiological trends and patterns. More details can be found at www.sogoresearch.com/epidemiology.
  • Real-Time Smart Feedback System for Effective Course Evaluation: A system designed to enhance the feedback process in educational settings, allowing for more timely and effective evaluations. Visit www.rteval.com for more information.
  • Web-Based Learning Management System: Developing a comprehensive platform, www.sograder.com, that facilitates online learning and assessment, catering to the evolving needs of modern education.

Future Courses (Planned for 2024)

  • Applied Large Language Models:This course focuses on the practical applications of Large Language Models (LLMs) across various domains. Students will study the fundamentals of LLMs, including architectures like GPT-3, BERT, and T5, and their deployment in text generation, semantic search, and chatbots. Emphasizing hands-on experience, the course will guide students through using and fine-tuning pre-trained models for specific applications, equipping them with the skills to address real-world challenges.
  • Advanced Topics in Large Language Models:This course delves into cutting-edge research and advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs), focusing on their role in Natural Language Processing (NLP). Covering seminal and contemporary papers, students will learn about the technical underpinnings, evolving capabilities, fine-tuning approaches, system design considerations, and ethical and security implications of LLMs. The course includes a significant research project, offering students a chance to engage practically with this evolving technology.
